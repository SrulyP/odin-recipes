Lasagna

Description

A delicious homemade lasagna sauce recipe. This recipe is jam-packed with hidden vegetables, but with a delicious taste. This recipe is great to prepare in batches and for freezer meals since it freezes very well and creates many servings. I make this recipe once a month, and it creates 5 lasagnas. I bake one and the rest goes into the freezer.

Ingredients

Sauce

  • 2 packs lasagna sheets
  • 2-kilogram tomatoes
  • 2 large zucchinis
  • 2-3 bell peppers (any color)
  • 2 jalapeños (optional)
  • 500-gram portobello mushrooms, peeled
  • 1 head of garlic
  • 2 large white or vidalia onions
  • 1 large eggplant, peeled
  • 500-gram cauliflower
  • ¼th cup nutritional yeast (nooch)
  • 1 cup broth (any kind)
  • 2 tbsp paprika
  • 2 tsp salt and pepper
  • 3-4 cans of diced tomato (optional if making big batches)

Cheese

  • 1-kilogram mozzarella
  • 250-gram parmesan
  • 250-gram ricotta
  • 150-gram butter
  • 100-gram flour
  • 3 cups milk
  • 2 cups spinach (frozen or fresh)
  • 2 eggs

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F.
  2. Wash and dice all the vegetables.
  3. Bake all vegetables at 375° for about 45 minutes, or until they become very soft. The vegetables can be baked in batches.
  4. Turn off the oven.
  5. Blend all the vegetables together using a blender, adding the broth paprika, and nutritional yeast.
  6. If you choose to make a large batch, add the cans of diced tomato now. Otherwise, continue without adding them.
  7. Start making a bechamel sauce by cooking the butter and then adding the flour slowly, mixing until a roux is formed.
  8. Add the milk, mixing until it is fully incorporated and there are no chunks.
  9. Keep mixing until the roux thickens.
  10. Mix in the mozzarella, parmesan, ricotta, and spinach (if frozen - thaw and drain first).
  11. Let the mixture cool down, and once it has cooled, mix in the eggs.
  12. Preheat the oven to 400°F
  13. Take your baking dish, add some vegetable sauce, then cover with lasagna sheets.
  14. Place some of the cheese sauce, then add some of the vegetable sauce on top.
  15. Keep layering until you reach the top of your baking dish, making sure the last layer is a cheese sauce layer.
  16. Cover the baking dish(es) with silver foil.
  17. Bake the lasagna in the oven covered at 400°F for about an hour, then uncover and bake until crispy on top (about another 20 minutes).
